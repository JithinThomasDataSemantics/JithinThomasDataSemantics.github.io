Saint Arthelais

Virgin and patron against bodily ills, against illness , against sickness , Benevento, Italy , exiled people , sick people. Also the patron of kidnap victims along with St Dagobert II. She is recorded as having fled Constantinople, to escape the attentions of Byzantine Emperor Justinian. Arthelais was the daughter of Proconsul Lucius and his wife, Anthusa. When Arthelais fled from the emperor, she went to her uncle, Narses, in Benevento. En route she was kidnapped by highway men, but was miraculously freed after three days. Tradition states that the entire population of the city welcomed her. Arthelais died at the age of sixteen. Her feast is celebrated on March 3.
